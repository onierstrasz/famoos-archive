SCG / FAMOOS / Reengineering Patterns

This document collects a number of reengineering patterns explaining you possible approaches for reengineering (or reverse engineering) object-oriented legacy systems.
Don't forget to join the reengineering mailing list hosted at Edinburgh and to send us feedback about the patterns.

Current Status

The current reengineering patterns are all canditate reengineering patterns. This means that they need further iterations and to become full-blown reengineering patterns. Some of them have been workshopped at EuroPLOP'98 and EuroPLOP'99 but we are actively looking for other places to obtain feedback, that's why we make everything available on the web.

Type Check Elimination
These two patterns tell you how you might eliminate type-checks by exploiting polymorphic features in OO languages.
  • Most recent version as part of the FAMOOS handbook [PDF | compressed postcript]
  • Version as accepted on the Working Conference on Reverse Engineering 1999 (WCRE'99) [index]
  • Version after being workshopped at EuroPLOP'98 [index]
A Pattern Language for Reverse Engineering
This is an (incomplete) pattern language explaining you how to reverse engineer an object-oriented legacy system, going from the first contact you have with the system (i.e., Read All the Code in One Hour), up untill you are preparing to actually reengineer the system (i.e., Write the Tests).
  • Most recent version as part of the FAMOOS handbook [PDF | compressed postcript]
  • Version after being workshopped at EuroPLOP'99 [index]
Other Reengineering Patterns

The Future

We are currently rewriting these patterns to form a handbook on object-oriented reengineering. The working title is 'Object-Oriented Reengineering: A Pattern-based Approach' and it is expected to appear in the first quarter of 2001.

Submitting your own Reengineering Patterns

We are also interested in other reengineering patterns. So if you experience in applying reengineering patterns or with reengineering techniques write us about your experience. We welcome patterns from any author wishing to contribute. If you have an idea and you are looking for a co-author, we would also be interested.

Related Web Page

We are in contact with Perdita Stevens and Rob Pooley at University of Edinburgh. They are also working on reengineering patterns. So do not hesitate to register to their mailing list and to read their patterns at http://www.dcs.ed.ac.uk/home/pxs/reengineering-patterns.html.
